Column Push & Pull

You can make a pull on columns. It is easy to apply this, just add push- or pull- and the next of it is the breakpoint size.

3 column
4 column
6 column
12 column
HTML 
<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-3 push-xs-2 p-s">
        3 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-4 push-xs-3 p-s">
        4 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-6 pull-xs-1 p-s">
        6 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-12 pull-xs-2 p-s">
        12 column
    </div>
</div>

Column Offset

Offset in column is like pull-, but pull- will only move the column that targeted and likely to overlap other column(s). With using offset, all of the column(s) in the front of the targeted column will be moved relatively and sizing themself automatically.

3 column
4 column
6 column
12 column
HTML 
<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-3 offset-xs-7 p-s">
        3 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-4 offset-xs-10 p-s">
        4 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-6 offset-xs-2 p-s">
        6 column
    </div>
</div>

<div class="row text-center">
    <div class="column xs-12 offset-xs-1 p-s">
        12 column
    </div>
</div>

Container

Center your content by using container class. Don't worry about width dimension or viewport, your content will stay in the center, as long as you are not modifying it.

HTML 
<div class="container">
    <!-- content -->
</div>

Full Container

full-container spanning the width and makes your content centered.

HTML 
<div class="full-container">
    <!-- content -->
</div>
